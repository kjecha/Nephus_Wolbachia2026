# Nephus_Wolbachia2026
This repository contains the data and R code file used for data visualization and analysis from the manuscript ["Wolbachia facilitates the reproduction of a parthenogenetic ladybug"](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.64898/2026.06.16.732365v1) -Jecha et al., 2026  
  
This experiment focused on understanding the effects of Wolbachia on a parthenogenetic population of the ladybug Nephus voeltzkowi. Practically, this involved administering Rifampicin antibiotic via artificial diet to Wolbachia-infected, parthenogenetic females, and having controls consisting of untreated, parthenogenetic females and treated and untreated sexual females that we demonstrate have no Wolbachia infection. The experiment was split into two parts: a treatment period in which each treatment group received their respective antibiotic or control diet, followed by an observation period in which all groups were switched to a control diet to observe the longevity of the treatment. We recorded the number of eggs produced by each female at regular time points during the experiment and whether they hatched or showed some external visual signs of development. We also quantified the Wolbachia titer level at several points in the experiment to verify that the antibiotic was having an effect on the treated parthenogens and that the sexual females were uninfected with Wolbachia. Here, we include the data table of the egg details as well as the data table of the titer quantification used in the analyses for this manuscript.
